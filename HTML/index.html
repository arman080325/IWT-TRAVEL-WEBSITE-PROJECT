featured places

Burj Khalifa: Dubai, United Arab Emirates

While many Middle Eastern countries boast architectural superlatives, the tallest of the futuristic towers – and the tallest building in the world – is Burj Khalifa.

Grand Canyon: Arizona, United States

The Grand Canyon, located in Arizona, is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World. Its immense size and intricate landscape offer breathtaking views and a variety of outdoor activities.

Statue of Liberty: New York, United States

The Statue of Liberty, a gift from France to the United States, is an iconic symbol of freedom and democracy. Located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, it welcomes millions of visitors each year.

Berlin, Germany

Berlin, the capital of Germany, is a city that is rich in history and culture. From the iconic Brandenburg Gate to the remnants of the Berlin Wall, there are countless landmarks to explore.

Khwaeng Wat Arun, Thailand

Khwaeng Wat Arun, also known as the Temple of Dawn, is a stunning Buddhist temple located on the Thonburi west bank of the Chao Phraya River in Bangkok. Its iconic spires are adorned with colorful porcelain and offer breathtaking views of the river and city.

North Bondi, Australia

North Bondi is a beautiful beachside suburb in Sydney, Australia, known for its stunning beach, vibrant community, and outdoor lifestyle. It's a popular destination for surfers, sunbathers, and those looking to enjoy the coastal scenery.

Rome, Italy

Rome, the capital of Italy, is a city that is steeped in history and culture. From the ancient ruins of the Colosseum to the stunning art of the Vatican, there is no shortage of things to see and do in this iconic city.

Colosseum: Rome

Now the remains of an enormous carved marble ellipse, the Colosseum was commissioned in A.D. 72 by Emperor Vespasian as an amphitheater to entertain the masses.

Fuvahmulah, Maldives

The jagged, 14,692-foot Matterhorn peak resembles a flint ax head above the posh resort area of Zermatt. The Matterhorn is higher than most of the peaks in the Alps near the Italian border.

Pisa, Italy

The Leaning Tower of Pisa is a freestanding bell tower, known for its unintended tilt. It is part of the cathedral of the Italian city of Pisa and is a famous tourist attraction.

Forbidden City: Beijing, China

The Forbidden City is a palace complex in Beijing, China, that served as the home of emperors and the political center of Chinese government for almost 500 years. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its stunning architecture and rich history.

Mayan Ruins: Tulum, Mexico

The Mayan ruins of Tulum are a well-preserved ancient city located on a cliff overlooking the Caribbean Sea. It was a major port for the Mayan civilization and is known for its stunning coastal views and impressive archaeological sites.

Disney World: Orlando, Florida

Walt Disney World Resort, commonly referred to as Disney World, is an entertainment complex in the Orlando area. It is the flagship destination of Disney's worldwide theme park empire and is known for its four theme parks, water parks, and numerous hotels and attractions.

Iguazu Falls: Brazil and Argentina

Iguazu Falls is a massive waterfall system located on the border of Brazil and Argentina. It is one of the largest and most impressive waterfall systems in the world, known for its stunning beauty and diverse wildlife.

Sydney Opera House: Sydney, Australia

The Sydney Opera House is a multi-venue performing arts center in Sydney, Australia. It is one of the most famous and distinctive buildings of the 20th century and is known for its unique architectural design and stunning harbor views.

Petra: Jordan

Petra is a historical and archaeological city in southern Jordan, famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Mount Kilimanjaro: Tanzania

Mount Kilimanjaro is the highest mountain in Africa, located in Tanzania. It is a dormant stratovolcano and is known for its stunning landscapes, diverse ecosystems, and challenging trekking routes.

Great Wall of China: China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of various materials, built along the northern borders of China to protect against invasions. It is one of the most iconic symbols of China and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
